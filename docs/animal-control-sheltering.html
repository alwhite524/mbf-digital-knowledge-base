Service model

Beaumont does not operate its own full-service animal shelter

Beaumont Animal Control and animal sheltering are separate components of the City's overall animal-services system.

Animal Control personnel perform municipal field response, enforcement, investigations, and public-safety functions.

Animals requiring sheltering are handled through a contracted shelter provider rather than a City-operated full-service municipal animal shelter.

This contracted model allows Beaumont to maintain its own Animal Control operation while relying on outside shelter infrastructure for animal housing and related services.

Historical provider

Ramona Humane Society

Longstanding relationship

Ramona Humane Society served as Beaumont's contracted sheltering provider for many years.

The relationship supported the City's Animal Control operation by providing shelter infrastructure and services for animals handled through municipal field operations.

2021 agreement

In September 2021, Beaumont approved a multi-year animal shelter services agreement with Ramona Humane Society.

The agreement continued Beaumont's established practice of contracting for shelter services instead of operating a City-owned full-service shelter.

Organizational transition

From Ramona Humane Society to Haven Pet Center

The shelter organization historically known as Ramona Humane Society later transitioned to the Haven Pet Center identity.

Beaumont's sheltering relationship continued through this organizational transition, but changes in shelter operations required adjustments to the City's contractual arrangement.

The change in name should therefore not be interpreted as Beaumont suddenly establishing an entirely unrelated sheltering system. It represents an evolution of the organization involved in the City's existing contracted shelter model.

Current model

Haven Pet Center

Contracted shelter services

Beaumont's current sheltering structure is associated with Haven Pet Center.

The City continues to rely on contracted shelter capacity rather than maintaining a separate Beaumont municipal shelter.

Municipal responsibilities remain

Contracting shelter services does not transfer Beaumont's Animal Control responsibilities to the shelter.

City Animal Control personnel continue to perform municipal enforcement, field response, investigations, and related public-safety functions.

Operational workflow

How an animal moves from field response to sheltering

1

Field Response

Beaumont Animal Control responds to an incident involving a stray, injured, loose, aggressive, or otherwise qualifying animal.

2

Assessment

Animal Control determines the appropriate response based on ownership information, public safety, animal condition, applicable law, and the circumstances of the call.

3

Custody

When authorized and necessary, the animal may be taken into municipal custody.

4

Transport

Animals requiring sheltering are transported into the City's contracted shelter system.

5

Shelter Services

The contracted provider performs shelter-related functions according to the applicable agreement and operating policies.

Important service change

Owner surrenders are not part of the current standard model

One of the most significant documented changes to Beaumont's sheltering arrangement involves owner-surrender services.

Under changes made to the sheltering relationship in 2024, owner-surrender services were removed from the contracted service structure available through Beaumont.

This means an owner who simply wishes to surrender a personally owned animal is in a different situation from an animal entering the sheltering system through an Animal Control enforcement or field-response action.

Residents seeking voluntary surrender may therefore need to pursue alternative shelter, rescue, rehoming, or other available resources.

Why the distinction matters

Key distinction

Beaumont operates the Animal Control function directly but contracts for the shelter infrastructure supporting that function.

Financial structure

Sheltering is a contracted municipal expense

Beaumont's sheltering arrangement represents a recurring cost associated with providing Animal Control services.

Rather than financing and staffing its own full-service shelter, the City pays an outside provider for defined shelter services.

This structure converts a potentially significant municipal infrastructure and staffing responsibility into a contractual operating expense.
