Current project phase

Phase II has moved from planning and funding into active construction.

The Riverside County Transportation Commission, City of Beaumont, and Caltrans are delivering Phase II of the State Route 60/Potrero Boulevard Interchange project.

A groundbreaking ceremony was held April 28, 2026, formally marking the start of the construction phase.

RCTC currently identifies May 2026 as the start of construction, with the new interchange anticipated to open to motorists in 2028.

Groundbreaking marks the transition into construction

RCTC and the City of Beaumont jointly held the Phase II groundbreaking ceremony on April 28, 2026.

The event brought together transportation partners, elected officials, stakeholders, and community members.

The milestone was important because it represented the visible transition from years of engineering, funding, agreements, and project preparation into physical construction.

Major work

What Phase II construction includes

1

Freeway Ramps

Construction of six ramps creating direct connections between Potrero Boulevard and State Route 60.

2

Partial Cloverleaf

Development of the interchange geometry required for the six-ramp partial-cloverleaf configuration.

3

Potrero Boulevard

Roadway work supporting the six-lane Potrero Boulevard crossing and connections to the new ramps.

4

Western Knolls Avenue

Realignment of Western Knolls Avenue to integrate the surrounding street network with the interchange.

5

Supporting Infrastructure

Associated roadway, drainage, utility, traffic-control, landscaping, and transportation improvements required to complete the interchange.

Project schedule

Construction planned from 2026 through 2028

RCTC currently identifies the Phase II construction period as 2026 through 2028.

Construction began in May 2026, following the April groundbreaking.

The new interchange is currently anticipated to open to motorists in 2028.

That date should be understood as the current project schedule, not as a completed milestone. Construction schedules can change because of field conditions, weather, utility conflicts, material availability, regulatory requirements, or other factors.

Construction activity

Traffic conditions will change as work progresses

Construction of a new freeway interchange requires work within and adjacent to active transportation corridors.

Temporary traffic-control measures may include lane closures, shoulder closures, local roadway restrictions, ramp-related work, construction access, and other short-term impacts.

RCTC publishes project construction activity and closure information as work progresses.

Because construction conditions can change quickly, current closure information should be obtained from the official project source rather than treated as permanent information on this page.

Air quality review

Project not identified as an air-quality concern

A project-level air-quality conformity analysis was prepared for Phase II.

RCTC reports that the Transportation Conformity Working Group confirmed the project is not considered a Project of Air Quality Concern.

The public review and comment period for that analysis occurred in May 2025 as the project advanced toward construction.

Intelligence assessment

Construction is now the primary project risk and milestone track

For much of the Potrero Interchange's history, the central questions involved planning, funding, agreements, and whether Phase II would reach construction.

That threshold has now been crossed.

With Ames Construction under contract and construction underway, the key questions shift toward schedule, cost control, traffic impacts, field progress, and successful delivery of the final interchange configuration.

Beaumont Intelligence should therefore treat construction updates, closure notices, schedule revisions, contract amendments, and eventual opening milestones as the project's highest-priority continuing intelligence.
