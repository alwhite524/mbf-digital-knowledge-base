Local authority

The legal foundation of Animal Control

Beaumont Animal Control operates within a legal framework created by local municipal law together with applicable California law.

The Beaumont Municipal Code establishes local requirements affecting animal ownership, licensing, control of animals, public nuisances, dangerous animals, enforcement, and related responsibilities.

These provisions provide the local legal authority used by Animal Control personnel when responding to complaints, investigating violations, and enforcing animal-related regulations.

What the municipal framework regulates

Licensing

Local law establishes licensing requirements and supports identification, rabies compliance, enforcement, and recovery of lost animals.

Animal Restraint

Local requirements govern circumstances in which animals must be controlled or restrained and provide authority for responding to animals running at large.

3

Nuisances

Animal-related conduct may become subject to enforcement when it creates recurring nuisance conditions affecting neighboring properties or the public.
