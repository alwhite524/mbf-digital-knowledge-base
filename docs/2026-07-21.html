Executive summary

A meeting centered on infrastructure and capital investment

The July 21 agenda is heavier than average on long-term infrastructure, public facilities, and financial planning. The most consequential decisions involve Stewart Park Phase II, a $3.43 million citywide street rehabilitation contract, wastewater planning, Community Facilities District bond refunding, and amendments to the City’s five-year Capital Improvement Plan.

Action Items J.4 and J.5 · Parks / Capital Project

Stewart Park Phase II: grant agreement and final design work

What Council is being asked to do: Approve the Outdoor Recreation Legacy Program grant agreement and an amendment with PlaceWorks for final plans, specifications, and construction-related work for Stewart Park Phase II.

Why it matters: These are implementation steps, not merely another conceptual discussion. Approval would preserve grant funding and move the project closer to construction bidding and delivery.

BI Insight

The two items should be read together: one secures the funding framework, while the other advances the professional design work needed to build the project.

  • What is the complete Phase II budget after grant and local funding are combined?
  • What remains before a construction contract can be awarded?
  • What schedule and grant deadlines govern the next steps?

Action Item J.3 · Transportation / Construction

$3.43 million annual citywide street rehabilitation project

What Council is being asked to do: Award a $3,428,547 public works agreement to Matich Corporation for the FY 2025–26 annual street rehabilitation and maintenance project.

Why it matters: This contract determines where a substantial portion of the City’s near-term pavement investment will occur and when residents can expect construction impacts and improvements.

BI Insight

The most useful attachment for residents is the project street list and map. It shows which streets were selected and provides a basis for comparing this year’s work with the broader pavement-management backlog.

  • Which streets and neighborhoods are included?
  • How were they ranked and selected?
  • What is the construction schedule?

Action Item J.2 · Utilities / Long-Range Planning

Wastewater Master Plan update

What Council is being asked to do: Award a professional services agreement to Webb Associates to update the City’s Wastewater Master Plan.

Why it matters: The plan will shape future sewer capacity, infrastructure priorities, development assumptions, and capital spending.

BI Insight

Master plans often matter more than individual construction contracts because they establish the technical assumptions and priority projects used for years afterward.

Action Item J.1 · Finance / Community Facilities Districts

Refunding outstanding CFD bonds

What Council is being asked to do: Authorize refunding transactions intended to reduce borrowing costs and provide savings to affected property owners without extending existing bond terms or affecting the General Fund.

Plain English: This is refinancing existing debt, not creating a new Citywide tax or borrowing program.

BI Insight

The packet includes multiple districts and extensive bond documents. The practical question is the projected savings after issuance and professional costs are deducted.
