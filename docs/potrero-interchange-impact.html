Mobility & Regional Effects

Potrero Interchange Traffic & Impact

How the SR-60/Potrero Boulevard Interchange is intended to affect traffic flow, freight movement, emergency access, local circulation, growth, and regional mobility.

Regional transportation project The interchange is designed to improve freeway access and support long-term mobility in Beaumont and the San Gorgonio Pass.

Transportation purpose

Why a new interchange matters

The Potrero Interchange is intended to provide an additional connection between Beaumont's local roadway system and State Route 60.

That connection is designed to improve traffic distribution, support growth, strengthen freight movement, and provide more direct access to areas south of the freeway.

The project also gives the City and surrounding region another freeway access point between existing interchanges, which can help reduce pressure on other parts of the transportation network.

Traffic flow

Distributing trips across another freeway connection

Existing condition

Without a full interchange at Potrero Boulevard, trips originating in developing areas south of SR-60 may need to travel farther through the local street system to reach existing freeway access points.

With Phase II

The six-ramp interchange creates direct access between Potrero Boulevard and SR-60, allowing more trips to enter and exit the freeway closer to their origin or destination.

Expected mobility benefit

A new interchange can distribute traffic across more access points rather than concentrating every trip at existing freeway interchanges.

Freight movement

Supporting regional goods movement

Freight movement is one of the major transportation purposes associated with the Potrero Interchange.

The project's receipt of Trade Corridor Enhancement Program funding reflects its connection to freight mobility and regional transportation performance.

Improved freeway access can provide trucks serving industrial and commercial areas with more direct routes to State Route 60.

That can reduce the need for some freight traffic to circulate farther through local streets before reaching the freeway network.

Local streets

Potential relief for surrounding roadway corridors

A new freeway connection can influence traffic patterns beyond the interchange itself.

Trips that currently use other east-west or north-south routes to reach existing interchanges may shift toward Potrero Boulevard once the new freeway access becomes operational.

The degree of redistribution will depend on actual travel behavior, surrounding development, and future traffic conditions after opening.

Beaumont Intelligence therefore distinguishes between the project's intended congestion-relief role and future measured traffic outcomes.

Emergency access

Another connection to the regional freeway system

Additional freeway access can also improve transportation flexibility for emergency response.

A completed interchange provides another route between local roads and SR-60, which may be valuable when responding to incidents or when other transportation corridors are constrained.

The practical emergency-response benefit will depend on incident location, roadway conditions, and operational circumstances, but additional connectivity generally increases route options.

Western Knolls Avenue

Local circulation changes are part of the project

Phase II includes realignment of Western Knolls Avenue.

That work is important because the interchange cannot operate effectively in isolation from the surrounding local roadway network.

The realignment helps integrate the ramp system, Potrero Boulevard, and nearby streets into a coordinated circulation pattern.

Construction impacts

Short-term disruption before long-term benefit

Construction itself can temporarily create traffic impacts.

Lane restrictions, shoulder closures, construction traffic, local roadway work, and temporary traffic-control measures may affect drivers while Phase II is being built.

These short-term impacts should be distinguished from the project's intended long-term mobility benefits after completion.

Current closure and construction-impact information should always be checked through RCTC's official project updates because field conditions can change rapidly.

Environmental review

Air-quality conformity reviewed before construction

Phase II underwent project-level air-quality conformity review as part of its transportation and environmental process.

RCTC reports that the Transportation Conformity Working Group determined the project was not considered a Project of Air Quality Concern.

A public review and comment period was conducted in May 2025 before construction began.

Evidence standard

Intended benefits are not the same as measured outcomes

Transportation projects are commonly justified using projected benefits developed during planning and environmental review.

Those projections are important, but they are not the same thing as measured results after a project opens.

Beaumont Intelligence will distinguish between what the Potrero Interchange is intended to accomplish and what future traffic and performance data demonstrate after the interchange becomes operational.

That distinction is especially important for claims involving congestion relief, safety, freight movement, and travel-time improvements.

Intelligence assessment

The interchange is both a transportation and growth project

The Potrero Interchange is more than a new set of freeway ramps.

It is infrastructure intended to reshape how traffic moves between developing portions of Beaumont and the regional freeway system.

Its significance spans local circulation, freight movement, emergency access, development capacity, and regional mobility.

The most important next analytical step will come after the interchange opens: comparing the project's intended benefits with actual traffic, safety, freight, and development outcomes.
