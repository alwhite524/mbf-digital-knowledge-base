Potrero Interchange Funding

The state, regional, and local funding sources supporting Phase II of the SR-60/Potrero Boulevard Interchange project.

Major funding milestone The project secured $33.5 million through the SB 1 Trade Corridor Enhancement Program.
Overview Timeline Project Design Funding Council Actions Construction Traffic & Impact Sources
Funding structure

Phase II is financed through a combination of local, regional, and state transportation funding.

The Potrero Interchange is not funded through a single source.

Official project information identifies City of Beaumont Capital Improvement Program funds, Transportation Uniform Mitigation Fee revenues, Riverside County Transportation Commission participation, and SB 1 Trade Corridor Enhancement Program funds as major components of the project finance structure.

This blended funding model reflects the project's significance as both a local Beaumont infrastructure project and a regional transportation and freight-mobility improvement.

State investment

$33.5 million Trade Corridor Enhancement Program award

In June 2023, Beaumont announced that Phase II had secured $33.5 million through California's Trade Corridor Enhancement Program.

The program is administered through the California Transportation Commission and is supported by SB 1 transportation funding.

Beaumont described the award as funding that would support engineering, construction, and other necessary project expenses.

The award became one of the most significant financial milestones in moving Phase II from project development toward construction.

Regional funding

RCTC participation

The Riverside County Transportation Commission has played a central role in advancing Phase II.

A 2023 Beaumont transportation project fact sheet identified $8 million in RCTC funding in addition to other committed project resources.

RCTC later assumed a major project-delivery role in partnership with Beaumont and Caltrans as Phase II advanced toward construction.

This regional participation reflects the interchange's significance beyond Beaumont's municipal boundaries.

Development impact funding

Transportation Uniform Mitigation Fee

Transportation Uniform Mitigation Fee funding is another documented component of the Potrero Interchange finance structure.

Beaumont's 2023 transportation fact sheet identified $8 million in TUMF funding previously allocated to Phase II.

TUMF revenues are transportation-impact funds associated with regional growth and development.

Their use on the interchange reflects the project's role in providing transportation capacity needed to support growth throughout the San Gorgonio Pass.

Local funding

City of Beaumont Capital Improvement Program

The City of Beaumont also contributes local capital funding toward the project.

Current City and RCTC project information identifies Beaumont's Capital Improvement Program as one of the funding sources supporting Phase II.

Local participation is important because major state transportation awards often operate as part of a broader financing package rather than replacing all local and regional investment.

2023 funding picture

Project described as fully funded

An August 2023 City transportation fact sheet described Phase II as fully funded.

At that time, the City identified three major funding components:

  • $33.5 million in Trade Corridor Enhancement Program funding
  • $8 million in RCTC funding
  • $8 million in previously allocated TUMF funding

The project schedule and cost structure later continued to evolve, which is why more recent official sources report different total and construction-cost figures.

Grant obligation

State funds formally released for construction

By 2025, Beaumont reported that the California Transportation Commission had formally obligated the $33.5 million TCEP funding for Phase II.

The obligation of the funds represented an important step beyond the original award announcement because the money was then available to support construction activities.

That milestone helped move the project from financing and pre-construction requirements into the construction-delivery stage.

Current cost reporting

Why official project cost figures differ

Current official sources do not all display the same project-cost figure because they are describing different financial measures.

City of Beaumont

Beaumont's current project page identifies:

  • Total Project Cost: $79.3 million
  • Construction Cost: $58 million

RCTC

RCTC's current public project page identifies approximately $64 million as the construction investment.

RCTC separately reported a $48,925,559 construction contract award to Ames Construction, Inc.

Important distinction

Total project cost, construction investment, and awarded construction contract are different financial measures and should not be treated as interchangeable.

Construction contract

Ames Construction selected for Phase II

RCTC announced the award of the Phase II construction contract to Ames Construction, Inc.

The reported construction contract amount was $48,925,559.

This amount represents the awarded construction contract and should be distinguished from broader project-cost figures that may include engineering, administration, right-of-way, contingencies, support, and other project expenses.

Intelligence assessment

Funding was the critical bridge from planning to construction

The Potrero Interchange illustrates how major transportation infrastructure typically depends on layered financing rather than a single municipal funding source.

Beaumont committed local resources, regional transportation funding supported the project, development-impact revenue contributed additional capacity funding, and the $33.5 million TCEP award provided a major state investment.

The most important analytical distinction is that funding announcements, project budgets, construction-investment estimates, and awarded construction contracts are not the same financial measure.

Tracking those figures separately provides a clearer picture of how Phase II progressed from a planned improvement into an actively funded and constructed interchange.
