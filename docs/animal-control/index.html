Animal Control Intelligence Center

A public-record intelligence center documenting the operations, history, contracts, legal framework, sheltering system, staffing, finances, and performance of Animal Control services in Beaumont, California.

About This Intelligence Center

The Animal Control Intelligence Center organizes official records and verified research concerning the City of Beaumont's Animal Control program.

The center is designed to help residents understand how Animal Control operates, how services are funded, which organizations provide supporting services, how the program has changed over time, and what questions remain unanswered.

Animal Control operates within the Beaumont Police Department. The City's current model combines municipal field operations and enforcement with contracted sheltering and licensing services.

Key Findings

  • Beaumont Animal Control operates within the Beaumont Police Department.
  • Beaumont historically operated as a regional Animal Control provider through agreements involving other jurisdictions.
  • The documented Beaumont-Calimesa Animal Control service relationship began in 2010 and concluded in 2023.
  • Beaumont does not operate its own full-service municipal animal shelter.
  • Animal sheltering is provided through a contracted service model currently associated with Haven Pet Center.
  • Licensing administration is supported through an outside service provider.
  • Available budget records show Animal Control staffing declined from 3.0 authorized FTE in FY 2018 to 2.0 FTE by FY 2019.
  • The reason for that staffing reduction remains an identified research gap.
  • Available performance reporting documents more than 2,000 Animal Control calls for service annually in recent periods reviewed.

Research Status

The foundational Animal Control investigation, AC-001, is substantially complete.

This Intelligence Center will continue to expand as additional official records, historical documents, and operational data become available.

Known evidence gaps are documented in the Source Register so future investigations can build on completed research rather than repeat it.

Research Standard

Beaumont Intelligence prioritizes primary government records, including municipal code, executed agreements, ordinances, resolutions, adopted budgets, City Council records, and official departmental publications.

Where evidence is incomplete, the limitation is identified rather than filled with assumptions.
